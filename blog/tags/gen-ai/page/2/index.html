Addressing AI Hallucinations in Medical Writing

September 25, 2024 · 4 min read
Hunter Shen
generative AI evangelist

In the rapidly evolving landscape of artificial intelligence, the term "hallucination" has taken on a new meaning. When prompted about topics they don’t know, large language models (LLMs) tend to generate responses that may appear authoritative and plausible to end users, yet they may be completely incorrect or contain serious errors. This phenomenon, known as "hallucination," is a common bias inherent in LLMs. In sectors such as Life Sciences, where accuracy is paramount, addressing this issue is critical.

Meet AuroraPrime Create by AlphaLife Sciences, an AI-powered medical writing software designed to mitigate these risks and ensure the highest level of accuracy in medical documentation.

Understanding AI Hallucination​

AI hallucination occurs when an AI algorithm produces results that are systemically prejudiced due to erroneous assumptions in the machine learning process. This can lead to the generation of incorrect or misleading information, which is particularly dangerous in the medical field. For instance, an AI might generate a plausible-sounding but incorrect dosage recommendation for a medication, potentially putting patients at risk.

One way to mitigate this phenomenon is through Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG). RAG involves automatically retrieving documents in a knowledge base that are most likely to contain elements of the response and adding them to the prompt so that the LLM has more context to give a correct answer. This technique, along with fine-tuning and other approaches, can significantly reduce the risk of hallucination.

AuroraPrime Create: The Solution​

AuroraPrime Create leverages advanced AI technologies to provide a robust solution to the problem of AI hallucination. Here’s how:

Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)​

AuroraPrime Create employs RAG to enhance the accuracy of its outputs. By storing both internal and external study-related documents in a corporate knowledge base, the software enhances the LLM’s internal information representation. This ensures that the generated content is not only coherent but also factually accurate.

For example, when generating a Clinical Study Report (CSR), AuroraPrime Create automatically retrieves relevant study protocols, statistical analysis plans, and previous reports. This ensures that the CSR is consistent with existing documents and free from hallucinations.

Advanced Prompt Engineering and Customization​

AuroraPrime Create allows for advanced prompt engineering based on specific organizational needs. This customization ensures that the AI model is aligned with the unique requirements and standards of the organization, further reducing the risk of hallucination.

A pharmaceutical company can leverage custom prompts alongside its corporate knowledge base to generate content that aligns with specific guidelines for document structure and style. This approach ensures all generated documents adhere to the company’s standards and regulatory requirements.

Reuse and Repurpose of Existing Content​

AuroraPrime Create enables users to reuse and repurpose existing content from other documents. This not only maximizes productivity and consistency but also minimizes the chances of introducing errors or hallucinations.

When drafting a Clinical Study Report (CSR), for example, AuroraPrime Create can pull relevant sections from previously approved documents, ensuring consistency and accuracy across all documents.

Content Quality Control (QC) Mechanism​

AuroraPrime Create has in place an iterative process that ensures continuous improvement and delivers high-quality AI-generated content tailored to specific requirements.The AI content quality control process involves multiple steps to ensure that the AI-generated output meets a high standard of accuracy, consistency, and relevance:

  1. Run Test Cases: Execute various model-prompt combinations to explore content generation.
  2. Evaluate Output: Compare generated content against a golden dataset to assess accuracy and quality.
  3. Inspect Results: Visualize and review output to identify areas for improvement.
  4. Fine-tune Prompts: Adjust prompts based on evaluation, then re-run test cases.
  5. Iterate: Repeat until optimal content quality is achieved.

The Future of Medical Writing​

The implementation of AuroraPrime Create by AlphaLife Sciences represents a significant step forward in the field of medical writing. By addressing the issue of AI hallucination at its source, AuroraPrime Create ensures that medical documents are accurate, consistent, and reliable. This not only enhances the quality of medical research but also safeguards patient safety.

In conclusion, the cure for AI hallucination lies in leveraging advanced technologies like Retrieval Augmented Generation, advanced prompt engineering, content reuse/repurpose, and content quality control. AuroraPrime Create embodies these solutions, setting a new standard for AI-powered medical writing. As the life sciences industry continues to evolve, tools like AuroraPrime Create will play an increasingly vital role in ensuring the accuracy and reliability of clinical documentation.

At the prestigious DPharm event, Sharon Chen, CEO and co-founder of AlphaLife Sciences, took the stage to deliver a keynote that left the audience both inspired and intrigued. In a speech filled with vision and technical insight, Chen outlined how her company is revolutionizing clinical trials through AI-powered solutions that promise to drastically reduce costs, enhance accuracy, and speed up the delivery of life-saving medicines.

Chen’s message resonated deeply with an audience of pharma industry leaders and tech innovators who share a common challenge: the ever-rising costs and complexities of clinical trials. “Clinical trials are becoming more expensive and time-consuming, and the urgency to bring treatments to patients faster has never been greater,” Chen said. Her solution? Generative AI—an emerging technology that is already proving its transformative potential across various industries.

Transforming Clinical Trials with AI​

AlphaLife Sciences, headquartered in Singapore, is a four-year-old startup making waves globally for its innovative approach to clinical trial management. The company collaborates with leading pharmaceutical and CRO (Contract Research Organization) companies to bring generative AI solutions into clinical trial workflows. With prestigious partnerships including Google for Startups, Microsoft for Startups, Nvidia's Inception Program, and Johnson & Johnson’s JLab, AlphaLife is rapidly gaining traction as a leader in clinical trial innovation.

Chen explained that her company’s flagship platform, AuroraPrime, leverages generative AI to address some of the most critical pain points in clinical trials—specifically, medical writing and documentation. The platform is designed to automate the creation of complex reports like Clinical Study Reports (CSRs), cutting down the time and labor traditionally required for this critical task.

“In the medical writing process, teams face the challenge of consolidating information from various sources—internal data, public information, and external collaborations,” Chen remarked. “This is a time-consuming process prone to human error, but it’s one that can be dramatically improved with AI.”

A New Era of Efficiency and Accuracy​

During her keynote, Chen highlighted three pivotal areas where generative AI can drive change: first draft automation, efficient template configuration, and full lifecycle integration of documents. According to her, these innovations will not only accelerate the creation of clinical trial documents but also ensure higher quality and consistency.

“Everyone knows the power of tools like ChatGPT for generating content quickly,” Chen noted. “But in regulated scientific fields like clinical trials, quality is paramount. The content must be consistent, precise, and accurate—AI cannot afford to make mistakes here.”

AlphaLife's solution? A rigorous AI framework that teaches models how to produce scientifically accurate, regulation-compliant content for medical writers. The AI is trained to summarize complex protocols, analyze statistical data, and generate TFL summaries with a level of consistency that is critical in clinical trials. Importantly, medical writers remain at the heart of the process, using AI as a tool to assist but not replace their expertise.

Seamless Integration for Medical Writers​

A standout feature of the AuroraPrime platform is its seamless integration with the tools medical writers already use, such as Microsoft Word. “We wanted to make sure the transition to AI-powered solutions would be as smooth as possible for medical writers,” Chen said. “Our platform integrates directly into their existing workflow, which means they can continue working in the environments they are most comfortable with.”

Chen further explained how the platform makes it easy for medical writers to configure templates without needing to write any code. Once a template is set up, it can be reused across multiple documents, further streamlining the process and reducing manual effort. The platform even enables content reuse across different studies, saving significant time in the creation of new reports.

“We’ve designed AuroraPrime to handle the full lifecycle of clinical trial documents,” Chen explained. “From protocol creation to final submissions, AI is driving efficiencies at every step, reducing errors and accelerating the process.”

Eliminating Hallucinations: The AI Challenge​

A major concern within the AI community is the issue of “hallucination,” where AI models generate false or misleading content. Chen acknowledged this challenge, especially in the highly regulated pharmaceutical space. “Hallucination is a real issue when using general-purpose models,” she said. “But our solution is built differently. By training the AI with golden datasets, generated from historical clinical trial documents, we’ve minimized this risk.”

Chen’s team has developed an advanced quality control mechanism, ensuring that the AI’s outputs meet the strict scientific and regulatory standards required in clinical trial documentation. The framework allows medical writers to fine-tune AI outputs, providing prompts and examples that guide the model in real time. This flexibility ensures that every document produced meets the highest standards of accuracy and consistency.

AI’s Role in the Future of Pharma​

In her closing remarks, Chen was optimistic about the future of AI in healthcare. “AI is not just about cutting costs and saving time—although those are clear benefits,” she said. “It’s about improving the quality and reliability of the documents that are so critical in clinical trials. It’s about helping medical writers do their jobs better, and ultimately, it’s about bringing life-saving treatments to patients faster.”

Chen's keynote made it clear that AlphaLife Sciences is at the forefront of a critical transformation in the pharmaceutical industry. With generative AI, the tedious and error-prone process of clinical trial documentation is becoming faster, more efficient, and more reliable. As AlphaLife continues to innovate and push the boundaries of what AI can do, the future of clinical trials looks increasingly bright.

As the keynote wrapped up, attendees were invited to visit AlphaLife’s booth for a hands-on demonstration of the AuroraPrime platform. There was palpable excitement in the room, as industry professionals and innovators alike could see the potential of AI-driven solutions to address one of the most pressing challenges in modern drug development.
